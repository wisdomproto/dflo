Did you know there is a specific window of time in which a child can grow?
The timing and duration of treatment depend on the child's growth pattern, target height, and the progression of secondary sexual characteristics. However, if the timing is too late, it becomes difficult for a child to grow sufficiently even with growth treatment. We therefore recommend having an evaluation done at the appropriate time. Because the evaluation is separate from treatment, you can have it done without any significant burden.

SIDE EFFECTS
What are the side effects?
187 MEDICAL CONSULTING 16
Phase 4 clinical trials have demonstrated that there are no serious side effects when growth hormone is administered to patients.
Some children may experience mild side effects while using growth hormone, but these naturally disappear over time.
Known side effects include muscle and joint pain (which resolves over time), swelling (which subsides naturally), hypothyroidism (extremely rare), and headaches caused by increased intracranial pressure (symptoms disappear immediately once treatment is stopped).
We monitor for side effects through continuous, regular check-ups.

Q&A
Frequently Asked Questions
Q A
1. When is the best time to start?
In pediatric growth treatment, timing has the greatest impact on results. Generally, the best window is ages 8–12 for girls and 9–13 for boys, though this can vary from child to child. Whether treatment is possible and how effective it will be can be confirmed through a consultation.
2. What are the side effects?
Mild side effects (joint pain, swelling, headache, etc.) may appear early on, but they usually resolve by briefly pausing treatment. We repeat tests every 6 months to check for side effects, and through many years of treatment experience it has been confirmed that there are no side effects that persist into adulthood.
3. How much does it cost?
Treatment costs are billed on an annual basis. Generally, 2–3 years of treatment are needed. The cost includes growth hormone management, puberty-progression management, sleep management, growth nutrition management, and corrective-exercise coaching.
187 MEDICAL CONSULTING 18
Q A
4. How much will my child grow after treatment?
It depends on the duration of treatment. On average, about 3 years of treatment can yield roughly 8 cm of additional growth, and the effect varies depending on genetic factors, sex, lifestyle habits, and whether precocious puberty is present.
5. How is the success of treatment evaluated?
Every 6 months, X-rays are used to confirm how much more the child has matured compared to the average child of the same age (one who has not received treatment).
6. How long does treatment take?
Several years of treatment are required. Typically, with 2–3 years of treatment a child gets close to their target height. However, this can vary somewhat depending on the timing of treatment, sex, stage of pubertal development, bone age, and other factors.
7. What kind of treatment is needed?
We provide integrated management of every aspect related to growth, as follows.
Growth hormone
Balance of hormones that affect growth
Puberty-progression management
Sleep
Height-growth nutrition management
Height-growth exercise and body-axis corrective exercise
